🔐 1. Logging In & Creating Your Account

Creating an account

1
Go to the portal link provided by your administrator (e.g. http://yourportal.com).
2
Click "Create one" below the Sign In button.
3
Fill in your Business Name, Username, Email, and choose a Password (at least 6 characters).
4
Click "Create Account". You will be redirected to the login page.

Signing in

1
Enter your Username and Password.
2
Click "Sign In". You will land on your Clientboard.
Tip: Your username and password are case-sensitive. If you forget your password, contact your administrator to reset it.

Logging out

1
Click the red "Logout" button in the top-right corner at any time.

📊 2. Clientboard (Dashboard)

The Clientboard is your home screen. It gives you an instant overview of your entire business.

What you see

CardWhat it means
Total ProductsHow many different products are in your inventory.
Low Stock ItemsProducts where stock has dropped to or below your minimum level.
Out of StockProducts with zero stock remaining.
Active DebtorsCustomers who still owe you money.
Total Outstanding DebtThe total rand amount owed to you across all debtors.

Items Needing Attention table

Below the cards you will see a table of any products that are low or out of stock. Use this to quickly decide what to reorder. Click "View Purchase Orders" to act on it.

If all stock levels are fine, you will see a green "All stock levels are OK" message instead of the table.

📦 3. Inventory

The Inventory page is where you manage all your products — adding, editing, updating stock, and more.

Searching for a product

1
Start typing in the Search bar at the top of the inventory page.
2
A live dropdown will appear showing matching products with their stock level and status.
3
Click a result to go directly to that product, or press Enter to filter the full table.

Adding a new product

1
Click the blue "+ Add Product" button (top right).
2
Fill in the product details:
  • Product Code — a unique code or SKU (e.g. TYR001). Required.
  • Product Name — the full name of the product. Required.
  • Category — group similar products (e.g. "Wheels & Tyres").
  • Unit — how it is measured (e.g. PCS, KG, BOX). Default is PCS.
  • Current Stock — how many you have right now.
  • Min Level — the quantity at which you want to be warned to reorder.
  • Last Cost Price — what you paid for it last time (in Rands).
  • Supplier — who you buy it from.
3
Click "Save".

Editing stock or min level quickly (inline edit)

1
In the inventory table, find the product you want to update.
2
Click directly on the Stock number or the Min Level number in the row.
3
A small input box will appear. Type the new value.
4
Press Enter to save, or Escape to cancel.
You do not need to open the edit form just to update stock — clicking the number in the table is the fastest way.

Editing all product details

1
Click the pencil icon in the Actions column for the product.
2
Update any fields and click "Save".

Deleting a product

1
Click the red trash icon for the product.
2
A confirmation box will appear — click OK to confirm deletion.
Warning: Deleting a product is permanent and cannot be undone.

Understanding status badges

OKStock is above the minimum level. You are fine.
LOW STOCKStock has dropped to or below the minimum level. Time to reorder.
OUT OF STOCKStock is at zero. Needs immediate attention.

📥 4. Importing Stock from Another System

If you already have your inventory in another program (Excel, Google Sheets, QuickBooks, etc.), you can import it instead of entering everything manually.

Step 1 — Choose your import source

Click "Import CSV" on the Inventory page. You will see three options:

OptionWhen to use it
File UploadYou have downloaded a .csv or .xlsx file from your software onto your computer.
URLYour file is online — e.g. a Google Sheet published as CSV. Paste the link.
APIYour system has an API. Enter the endpoint URL and API key (ask your IT person for these).

Google Sheets — how to get the URL

1
Open your Google Sheet.
2
Click File → Share → Publish to web.
3
Set the format to Comma-separated values (.csv) and click Publish.
4
Copy the link and paste it into the URL field in the importer.
Alternatively, go to File → Download → Comma Separated Values, save the file, then use the File Upload option.

Step 2 — Map your columns

1
After uploading, you will see your file's column names and a preview of the first few rows.
2
For each app field (Name, Stock, Category, etc.), the system will try to auto-detect the matching column from your file.
3
Check each field. If a column was not detected, type the exact column name from your file, or click the field and pick from the list.
4
Only Product Name is required. Everything else is optional.
5
Click "Next: Review & Edit".

Step 3 — Review & Edit before importing

1
You will see every row in a fully editable table. Check that the data looks correct.
2
Click any cell to edit it directly.
3
Click the × button at the end of a row to remove it from the import. Click again to restore it.
4
Use "+ Add row" to add a blank product manually.
5
When satisfied, click "Import X Product(s)".

⚖️ 5. Setting Minimum Stock Levels

The minimum level tells the system when to flag a product as "Low Stock". When stock drops to or below this number, you get a warning.

Setting the min level for one product

1
In the inventory table, click directly on the Min Level number for any product.
2
Type the new value and press Enter.
The system remembers that you set this manually. When you use "Set Default Min Level", you can choose to skip products you have already set individually.

Setting the same min level for all products at once

1
Click the "Set Default Min Level" button at the top of the Inventory page.
2
In the popup, type the number you want (e.g. 5).
3
At the bottom of the popup, you will see a checkbox: "Also apply to products I manually changed from the table".
  • Unchecked — only products you have never individually set will be updated. Your manually-set ones stay as they are.
  • Checked — every product including ones you set manually will be changed to this number.
4
Click "Apply".

🛒 6. Purchase Orders

The Purchase Orders page automatically lists every product that is at or below its minimum stock level — these are the products you need to reorder.

Viewing what needs to be reordered

1
Click "Purchase Orders" in the left sidebar.
2
You will see a table showing all products that need attention, including their current stock and minimum level.
3
Use this list to contact your supplier and place your order.
This list updates automatically as stock changes. Once you reorder and update stock levels in Inventory, the product will disappear from this list.
Products only appear here if their minimum level is set above 0. Make sure you have set min levels in your Inventory first.

👥 7. Debtors

The Debtors section helps you track customers who owe you money and send them payment reminders automatically.

Adding a debtor

1
Click "Debtors" in the sidebar, then click "+ Add Debtor".
2
Fill in:
  • Name — the customer's full name. Required.
  • Phone — their phone number (used for WhatsApp reminders).
  • Email — their email address (used for email reminders).
  • Amount Owed — how much they owe in Rands.
  • Date of Purchase — when the sale happened.
  • Products Owed — what they bought (e.g. "2x Mag Wheels").
  • Reminder Method — how to contact them: Email, WhatsApp, or Both.
  • Reminder Days — how many days after purchase to send the first reminder.
3
Click "Save".

Sending a reminder manually

1
On the Debtors page, find the customer in the table.
2
Click the "Send Reminder" button in their row.
3
A reminder will be sent immediately via the method set on their record (email or WhatsApp).

Marking a debtor as paid

1
Click the pencil icon to edit the debtor.
2
Tick the "Paid" checkbox and click "Save".
3
The debtor will be marked as paid and removed from the active count on your Clientboard.

🔔 8. Notifications

The Notifications page lets you send instant low-stock alerts and view a history of all alerts sent.

Sending a stock alert

1
Click the Send Alert button in the top navigation bar.
2
The system will immediately send an alert listing all low-stock and out-of-stock products to your configured recipients.

Viewing notification history

1
Click "Notifications" in the sidebar.
2
You will see a full log of every notification sent — including the date, recipient, and whether it succeeded or failed.
To set up who receives alerts (email address, WhatsApp number), go to Settings.

⚙️ 9. Settings

Settings lets you configure how alerts are sent, who receives them, and customise the reminder messages.

Opening Settings

1
Click "Settings" at the bottom of the left sidebar.

What you can configure

SettingWhat it does
Alert EmailThe email address that receives low-stock alerts.
WhatsApp NumberThe number that receives WhatsApp alerts via CallMeBot (include country code, e.g. +27).
CallMeBot API KeyThe key from CallMeBot needed to send WhatsApp messages. See callmebot.com for setup instructions.
Notification MessageThe text of the low-stock alert message. Can include placeholders for product details.
Debt Reminder TemplateThe message sent to debtors as a payment reminder.
1
Update the fields you want to change.
2
Click "Save Settings".
WhatsApp alerts require a free CallMeBot account. Visit callmebot.com, follow the setup steps, and paste your API key here.
